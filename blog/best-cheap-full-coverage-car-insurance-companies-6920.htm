Best Cheap Full Coverage Car Insurance Companies

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Finding affordable full coverage car insurance can feel like searching for a needle in a haystack—especially with rising inflation, unpredictable fuel prices, and increasing repair costs. However, securing reliable yet budget-friendly coverage is not impossible. Whether you're a new driver, a budget-conscious family, or someone looking to switch providers, this guide highlights the best cheap full coverage car insurance companies in 2024.

What Is Full Coverage Car Insurance?

Full coverage car insurance isn’t a single policy but rather a combination of coverages that typically include:

  • Liability Insurance – Covers damages you cause to others.
  • Collision Insurance – Pays for repairs to your car after an accident.
  • Comprehensive Insurance – Protects against non-collision incidents (theft, vandalism, natural disasters).
  • Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist Coverage – Steps in if the at-fault driver lacks sufficient insurance.

While full coverage costs more than minimum liability, it provides far better financial protection.

Top 5 Cheap Full Coverage Car Insurance Companies

1. Geico – Best for Budget-Conscious Drivers

Geico consistently ranks as one of the cheapest full coverage providers, thanks to its low overhead costs and strong digital presence.

Why Choose Geico?

  • Competitive rates for safe drivers and military members.
  • Easy-to-use mobile app for claims and policy management.
  • Discounts for bundling, good students, and defensive driving courses.

Average Annual Cost: ~$1,200

2. State Farm – Best for Personalized Service

State Farm’s vast network of local agents makes it a great choice for drivers who prefer a human touch.

Why Choose State Farm?

  • Strong customer satisfaction ratings.
  • Usage-based Drive Safe & Save program lowers premiums.
  • Multiple discount opportunities (multi-car, accident-free).

Average Annual Cost: ~$1,300

3. Progressive – Best for High-Risk Drivers

If you have a less-than-perfect driving record, Progressive offers some of the most affordable full coverage options.

Why Choose Progressive?

  • Name Your Price® tool helps customize plans within budget.
  • Snapshot® program rewards safe driving habits.
  • Competitive rates for drivers with past violations.

Average Annual Cost: ~$1,350

4. USAA – Best for Military Families

Exclusively for military members, veterans, and their families, USAA delivers unbeatable rates and top-tier service.

Why Choose USAA?

  • Exceptionally low premiums and high customer satisfaction.
  • Free accident forgiveness for long-term customers.
  • Comprehensive benefits tailored to military life.

Average Annual Cost: ~$1,100 (for eligible members)

5. Erie Insurance – Best for Regional Value

Operating in 12 states, Erie Insurance offers some of the cheapest full coverage rates with outstanding local service.

Why Choose Erie?

  • Low complaint ratios and high financial stability.
  • Accident forgiveness and diminishing deductibles.
  • Bundling discounts for home and auto policies.

Average Annual Cost: ~$1,250

How to Get the Cheapest Full Coverage Rates

Compare Multiple Quotes

Insurance rates vary drastically between providers. Always get at least three quotes before committing.

Increase Your Deductible

Opting for a higher deductible (e.g., $1,000 instead of $500) can significantly lower your premium.

Take Advantage of Discounts

Common discounts include:
- Safe driver (no accidents/tickets).
- Multi-policy (bundling home and auto).
- Good student (for young drivers with strong grades).

Maintain a Good Credit Score

In most states, insurers use credit-based insurance scores to determine rates. Improving your credit can lead to lower premiums.

Drive Less with Usage-Based Insurance

Programs like Progressive’s Snapshot or State Farm’s Drive Safe & Save reward low-mileage and safe driving habits.

The Impact of Global Trends on Car Insurance Costs

Rising Inflation & Supply Chain Issues

Car repairs are more expensive due to parts shortages and labor costs, pushing insurance premiums upward.

Electric Vehicles (EVs) & Insurance

While EVs save on fuel, their advanced tech makes repairs pricier, affecting insurance rates. Some insurers now offer EV-specific discounts.

Climate Change & Natural Disasters

Increasing floods, wildfires, and hurricanes mean comprehensive coverage is more critical—and sometimes more expensive—than ever.

Autonomous Driving Technology

As cars get smarter, insurers adjust pricing models. Features like automatic braking may lower premiums, while expensive sensors could raise them.

Final Thoughts

Securing cheap full coverage car insurance requires research, but the right provider can save you hundreds annually. Whether you prioritize digital convenience (Geico), personalized service (State Farm), or military benefits (USAA), there’s an affordable option for every driver. Stay informed, compare quotes, and leverage discounts to maximize savings without sacrificing protection.
