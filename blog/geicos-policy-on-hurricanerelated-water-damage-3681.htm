GEICO’s Policy on Hurricane-Related Water Damage

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Hurricanes are becoming more frequent and intense due to climate change, leaving homeowners and drivers vulnerable to devastating water damage. As one of the largest auto insurers in the U.S., GEICO has specific policies addressing hurricane-related water damage. Understanding these policies can save you time, money, and stress when disaster strikes.

How GEICO Covers Water Damage from Hurricanes

GEICO’s approach to hurricane-related water damage depends on the type of insurance policy you hold—auto, homeowners, or renters. Here’s a breakdown of what’s typically covered and what’s not.

Auto Insurance and Flood Damage

If your car is damaged by floodwaters during a hurricane, GEICO’s comprehensive auto insurance is what you’ll need. This optional coverage protects against non-collision incidents, including:
- Flooding
- Falling debris (e.g., tree branches)
- Storm surges

Key points to remember:
- Liability and collision coverage do not cover flood damage.
- If you live in a hurricane-prone area, adding comprehensive coverage is highly recommended.
- Claims must be filed promptly—delays can complicate the process.

Homeowners and Renters Insurance

For homeowners, GEICO partners with third-party insurers to provide property coverage. Standard homeowners insurance typically covers:
- Wind damage (e.g., roof destruction)
- Water damage from rain entering through a damaged roof

However, flood damage is almost always excluded from standard policies. You’ll need a separate flood insurance policy, usually through the National Flood Insurance Program (NFIP).

Renters should note:
- Your landlord’s insurance does not cover your personal belongings.
- GEICO’s renters insurance may cover storm-related water damage, but flood damage requires additional coverage.

Filing a Claim After a Hurricane

If your property or vehicle suffers hurricane-related water damage, follow these steps to file a claim with GEICO:

1. Document the Damage Immediately

Take photos and videos of all affected areas before making temporary repairs. This evidence is crucial for your claim.

2. Contact GEICO ASAP

File your claim online, via the GEICO mobile app, or by calling their 24/7 claims hotline. The sooner you report, the faster your claim can be processed.

3. Prevent Further Damage

GEICO expects policyholders to take reasonable steps to mitigate additional damage (e.g., covering broken windows, moving valuables to a dry area). Keep receipts for any emergency repairs—they may be reimbursable.

4. Work with an Adjuster

A GEICO claims adjuster will assess the damage. Be prepared to provide:
- Proof of ownership (receipts, photos)
- A detailed list of damaged items
- Any relevant police or weather reports

Common Misconceptions About Hurricane Coverage

Many policyholders assume their insurance will cover all hurricane-related losses, but that’s not always true. Here are some myths debunked:

Myth 1: "My Auto Insurance Covers All Hurricane Damage"

Reality: Only comprehensive coverage includes flood damage. If you only have liability insurance, you’re out of luck.

Myth 2: "Homeowners Insurance Covers Flooding"

Reality: Most policies exclude flood damage. Separate flood insurance is a must in high-risk zones.

Myth 3: "I Can File a Claim Whenever I Want"

Reality: Delaying a claim can lead to denial. Report damage as soon as it’s safe to do so.

Preparing for Hurricane Season

Since hurricanes are unpredictable but inevitable in certain regions, proactive measures can minimize losses:

For Your Car

  • Park in a garage or elevated area if possible.
  • Consider comprehensive coverage if you don’t already have it.

For Your Home

  • Install storm shutters or reinforce windows.
  • Review your insurance policy annually to ensure adequate coverage.

Emergency Kit Essentials

  • Important documents (insurance policies, IDs) in a waterproof container.
  • Backup power sources (batteries, portable chargers).

By understanding GEICO’s policies and taking preventive steps, you can navigate hurricane season with greater confidence. Stay informed, stay prepared, and ensure your coverage meets your needs before the next storm hits.
