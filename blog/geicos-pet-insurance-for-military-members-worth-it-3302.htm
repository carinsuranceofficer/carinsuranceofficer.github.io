GEICO’s Pet Insurance for Military Members: Worth It?

Home / Blog / Blog Details

Military members face unique challenges, from frequent relocations to unpredictable deployments. Amid these demands, caring for a pet can be both a source of comfort and an added responsibility. With veterinary costs rising and pet ownership becoming more expensive, GEICO’s pet insurance for military families has emerged as a potential solution. But is it worth it? Let’s break down the benefits, costs, and real-world value of this offering.

The Rising Cost of Pet Care in America

Pet ownership isn’t cheap. In 2023, the average annual cost of owning a dog reached $1,400–$2,800, while cats averaged $900–$1,500. Unexpected emergencies—like surgeries, chronic illnesses, or accidents—can easily add thousands more. For military families, who often move across states or overseas, accessing consistent and affordable veterinary care adds another layer of complexity.

Why Pet Insurance Matters for Military Families

Military life is unpredictable. A sudden PCS (Permanent Change of Station) or deployment can disrupt routines, including pet care. Here’s why pet insurance can be a game-changer:

  • Financial Protection: Emergency vet bills can derail a budget. Insurance helps mitigate surprise costs.
  • Access to Care: Some bases have limited veterinary services, forcing families to seek off-base care, which may be pricier.
  • Peace of Mind: Knowing your pet is covered allows you to focus on duty without worrying about unexpected expenses.

How GEICO’s Pet Insurance Works

GEICO doesn’t underwrite pet insurance directly but partners with Embrace Pet Insurance to offer coverage. Here’s what military families should know:

Coverage Options

GEICO’s pet insurance (via Embrace) offers customizable plans, including:

  • Accident & Illness Coverage: Covers injuries, illnesses, diagnostics, and treatments (e.g., cancer, infections).
  • Wellness Rewards: Optional add-on for routine care (vaccinations, dental cleanings).
  • Prescription Coverage: Includes medications for chronic conditions.

Key Features

  • No Network Restrictions: Use any licensed vet in the U.S. or abroad (great for overseas assignments).
  • Fast Reimbursements: Claims are processed in 5–15 days, with direct deposit options.
  • Military Discounts: Some plans offer discounts for active-duty members and veterans.

Costs and Deductibles

Pricing varies by pet age, breed, and location, but here’s a rough estimate:

  • Dogs: $30–$70/month
  • Cats: $15–$40/month
  • Deductibles: $200–$1,000 annually

While not the cheapest option, GEICO’s plans are competitive, especially with potential military discounts.

Comparing GEICO to Other Military-Friendly Pet Insurers

How does GEICO stack up against other providers catering to military families?

USAA’s Pet Insurance

USAA partners with Pets Best, offering similar coverage but often at lower premiums. However, GEICO’s Embrace partnership provides more flexible wellness add-ons.

Nationwide Pet Insurance

Nationwide covers exotic pets (e.g., birds, reptiles), which GEICO doesn’t. But Nationwide’s premiums can be higher for standard pets.

ASPCA Pet Health Insurance

ASPCA offers robust coverage but lacks military-specific perks. GEICO’s partnership with Embrace may provide better customer service for military families.

Real-Life Scenarios: Is GEICO’s Plan Worth It?

Case 1: The Deployment Dilemma

Imagine a Marine stationed in Okinawa with a Labrador Retriever. Local vet costs are high, and emergency care is limited. GEICO’s no-network policy means they can visit any vet and still get reimbursed—critical for overseas duty stations.

Case 2: The Cross-Country PCS

An Army family relocating from Texas to Germany faces a 12-hour flight with their cat. If the cat develops stress-induced illness mid-move, GEICO’s coverage could save them thousands in emergency vet bills.

Case 3: The Aging Military Working Dog

Retired MWDs (Military Working Dogs) often develop joint issues. GEICO’s chronic condition coverage helps offset long-term medication and physical therapy costs.

Potential Drawbacks

No plan is perfect. Here are some cons to consider:

  • Pre-Existing Conditions: Like most insurers, GEICO doesn’t cover them.
  • Waiting Periods: 14 days for illnesses, 48 hours for accidents.
  • Breed Exclusions: Some high-risk breeds may face higher premiums.

Final Verdict: Should Military Families Enroll?

For military members who prioritize flexibility and comprehensive coverage, GEICO’s pet insurance is a strong contender. While it may not be the absolute cheapest, the lack of network restrictions and potential discounts make it a practical choice—especially for those facing frequent moves or overseas assignments.

Before enrolling, compare quotes, read reviews, and consider your pet’s specific needs. For many military families, the peace of mind alone could be worth the investment.
